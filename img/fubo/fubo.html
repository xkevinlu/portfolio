Problem



In 2016, FuboTV raised a large Series B round and struck deals with major television networks to scale their live soccer internet streaming service into a full-blown cable replacement. Due to the bundling nature of the network deals, we were sitting on a mountain of premium live-TV streams including FOX, NBC, ABC, etc. and needed a way to market it to new users.


Early sketches for co-watching on YouTube

My Role & Process



I joined the team together at the same time as two other designers for a full design team of four. Working closely with product owners and the other designers, I took the responsibility of designing a new user funnel that would communicate the vast new product offering FuboTV would now have.

  • 1.

    Foundational Research


    Our team started with understanding our target users - teens. I helped organize and attended several in-home user sessions led by a freelance UX Researcher, and gathered insights.

  • 2.

    Design Sprint &
    Defining Key Opportunities


    Our team did a 2 week long design sprint during which our team reviewed research findings and ideated on solutions. I led the creation of concept videos to illustrate our proposed product direction.

  • 3.

    Information Architecture


    Worked with PM & Eng to map out user flows for both the sharing and video recieving experience.

  • 4.

    Design Iterations using the Lean UX methodology


    I planned out one week design sprints with my UX researcher. We tested flows and iterated on them each week for 2 months.

  • 5.

    Prototypes, High fidelity mocks, illustrations & motion design


    During our Lean UX sprints, I created prototypes using framer. After converging on an ideal solution for the designs, I created all final assets, and managed the work of motion and visual designers.

  • 6.

    Marketing video


    To prepare for launch, I provided art direction on our marketing video, which was released with our launch announcement.

  • 7.

    Additional Features


    After launching, we continued to test and ideate on new features.

Solution



Our team's goal was to make YouTube the best place to share and chat about video with friends. We did this by first creating a direct sharing feature within YouTube, then incorporating a co-watching experience within it, allowing users to watch videos and converse at the same time.

Impact



We launched native sharing in May 2016, with positive media response and user engagement. Most importantly, it created a viral social graph within YouTube, building a foundation for future social features.
